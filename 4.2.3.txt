	Some labial words are also registered under SR /-ɨw/ but CPR validates
only the readings with /-wɨ/. LR and RR transcriptions keep distinction
between /ɱwɨ/ and /wɨ/. SR /ŋwɨʔ/ has PR /wɨʔ/. Some *rusheng* words whose
Middle Chinese ending was *-k have CPR /-jɨw/.

	1. This word occurrs only once in the *Pônyôk Pak t`ongsa*. I do not
know whether it is a misprint for *mu* or a forerunner of the change: ɱwɨ >

	3. This word occurrs twice in the *Pônyôk Pak t`ongsa*. Is this LR /wɨ/

	4. We do not know whether this RR meant to be /ʂwɔ/ or /swɔ/, for the
