	Most retroflex and sibilant *rusheng* words, whose Middle Chinese ending
was *-k, have PR variant /wɨʔ/, which generally agree with Modern
Mandarin. Some *rusheng* words whose Middle Chinese ending was *-k have CPR
variant /-jɨw/.

Words	Tone	SR	PR	CPR	LR	RR	Mo.M
{}

	1. This word occurrs only once in the *Pônyôk No Gôltae*. It is not
clear why its LR and RR still retains a medial /-j-/. Is it a misprint? Or
does it indicate that two forms, /tʂjwɨʔ/ and /tʂwɨʔ/ for 囑, coexist in
actual speech?
	2. Variant readings for this word such as *yangping* /tʂjɨw/ and *qu*
/tʂjɨw/ are also noted in the general illustrations of the *No Gôltae* and
the *Pak t`ongsa*.
