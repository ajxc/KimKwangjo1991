====182

	The note for the the non-*rusheng* final *we* /-wɔ/ says: "The current
practice follows *o* /wɔ/ regardless of initials". This transcriptional
change is a reflection of Ch`we Sejin's new phonetic interpretation on
Middle Mandarin /wɔ/. Hence, it has nothing to do with a phonemic change of
Middle Mandarin /wɔ/. The LR follows -*we* and the RR, -*o*, including the *ru*
words. The initial /ŋ/ is not distinct in PR.

	1. Of a total of five occurrences, four of them are LR /mwɔʔ/ and RR
/mwɔ/; one, LR /maʔ/ and RR /ma/.
	2. The *No Pak chimnam* gives :*wa* /wa/ (voiced *ping*) as a popular
reading.
