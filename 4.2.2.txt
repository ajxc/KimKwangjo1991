	The SR /ɱjɨj/ has CPR _ɱui_ and RR _∅ui_, which are phonemically
interpreted /wɨj/. This means that by the early sixteenth century /ɱjɨj/
merged into /wɨj/. Its process may be portrayed as follows: /ɱjɨj/ > */mjɨj/
> /wɨj/. The other labidentals /fjɨj/ and /vjɨj/, however, developed a
depalatized CPR and/or RR variant /fɨj/ and/or /vɨj/ only in the _ping_ words
as we see in the list below. These may be considered forerunners of these
changes. For the _shang_ and _qu_ words seem to have lost /-j-/ as late as in
the eighteenth century. (For this, look at Chapter Four, 5.2.1.2
“Depalatization”.) Many of bilabial non-_rusheng_ words have PR or CPR
/-ɨj/, which agree mostly with Modern Mandarin but with a few exceptions.
Many of retroflex non-*rusheng* words have PR /-ɨ/. These may be considered
forerunners because all the retroflex words with /-jɨ/ have eventually
evolved into /-ɨ/ in Modern Mandarin. The words with SR /ŋjɨ/ have PR /jɨ/,
but two *ru* words 逆 and 疑 with SR /jɨ/ reversely have PR /ŋjɨʔ/.
